Lakers acquire Luka Doncic from Mavericks for Anthony Davis in blockbuster trade

By: Zapanta, Zeus Levi C.

The deal happened after the Los Angeles Lakers beat the New York Knicks Lakers 128 - Knicks 112 in Madison Square Garden, according to people with knowledge of the situation not authorized to speak publicly. The Lakers sent Anthony Davis, young guard Max Christie and their 2029 first-round draft pick to Dallas Mavericks for Luka Doncic and forwards Maxi Kleber and Markieff Morris.

Additional Context: The trade came as a shock to many fans who were not expecting the Lakers to make such a big move. This trade is seen as a bold decision that could redefine the Lakers' chances of winning another championship in the near future.

According to people with knowledge of the trade talks not authorized to discuss the deal, it occurred without the knowledge of any players, including LeBron James, and was done almost completely between general managers Rob Pelinka of the Lakers and Nico Harrison, who joined the Mavericks after being a longtime Nike executive. The two have an extensive history dating to Kobe Bryant’s time with the sneaker brand.

Insights: The deal happened quickly and without much prior discussion with the players, which raised some concerns among the Lakers’ fanbase. However, many believe this could be a strategic move for the future of the team, especially with Luka Doncic's potential to lead the team to success.

Questions about the future of LeBron James, who holds a no-trade clause and can opt out of a $52.6-million player option and become a free agent after this season, are being asked by executives on rival teams.

In Doncic, the Lakers have acquired one of the premier on-ball players in the world. He’s a five-time All-Star, a five-time All-NBA player who led the Mavericks to the NBA Finals a season ago. He’s averaging 28.1 points, 8.3 rebounds and 7.8 assists this season and is recovering from a calf injury that’s kept him out since Christmas. He’s expected back before the All-Star break.

Davis, an NBA champion with the Lakers in 2020 and in the middle of another All-Star season, rejoins an assistant from that championship team in Mavericks coach Jason Kidd and, like Pelinka, has extensive ties with Harrison through Nike. Davis joins a Mavericks team that has Kyrie Irving, Klay Thompson and an emerging two-way player in Christie, who has played the best basketball of his career.
